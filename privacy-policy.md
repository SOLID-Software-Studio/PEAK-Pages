# Privacy Policy

Last updated: May 8, 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how PEAK ("the App") handles personal data. The App is provided by Julian Ewald and Felix Binder, both based in Austria ("we", "us", or "our").

If you have questions about this Privacy Policy, contact us at privacy@solid-software.studio

## 1. Summary

PEAK is a workout tracking app. The current alpha version is distributed to a closed group of testers in Austria and/or the European Union. The App is designed to store workout, exercise, routine, and settings data locally on your device. We do not sell personal data.

## 2. Data We Process

Depending on how you use the App, the following data may be processed:

- Workout data, such as workout history, exercises, routines, sets, reps, weight, distance, duration, effort, and weekly goals.
- App settings, such as language, preferred units, and onboarding status.
- Feedback data, if you contact us or use a feedback link, such as your message, email address, and technical context you choose to provide.
- Crash report data, if you choose to send an error report, such as error messages, stack traces, app version, device model, operating system version, runtime information, timestamps, and related technical diagnostics.
- Optional report content, if the error report screen lets you add comments or contact details, such as the message and contact information you choose to provide.
- Alpha distribution and update data processed through Expo/EAS, such as technical information needed to distribute app builds or updates, including operating system information, project identifiers, and random installation/update tokens.

## 3. How We Use Data

We use data to:

- Provide the App's core workout tracking features.
- Save your preferences.
- Investigate crashes, improve reliability, and fix bugs.
- Respond to support or feedback requests.
- Comply with legal obligations.

## 4. Local Storage

Workout and settings data are stored locally on your device. We do not operate a PEAK account system, cloud sync, or paid subscription for the current alpha version. We do not receive or access your local workout data unless you intentionally share it with us, for example in a support request or crash report.

## 5. Third-Party Services

The App may use third-party services for specific purposes:

- Alpha distribution, builds, and updates: Expo / EAS, provided by 650 Industries, Inc. ([https://expo.dev](https://expo.dev))
- Feedback: UserJot ([https://userjot.com](https://userjot.com))
- Crash reporting: Sentry, provided by Functional Software, Inc. ([https://sentry.io](https://sentry.io))

These providers may process data according to their own privacy policies. We only use service providers that are intended to provide appropriate protection for user data.

### Expo/EAS Alpha Distribution

We use Expo Application Services (EAS) to build, distribute, and update the alpha version of the App. Expo may process limited technical data necessary for these services, such as app project identifiers, operating system information, and random tokens used to determine whether an installation has requested or downloaded an update.

### Sentry Crash Reports

When an error occurs, the App may show a crash report prompt. A report is sent to Sentry only if you choose "Send report" or a similar confirmation action. If you choose "Cancel", the report is not intentionally sent by the App.

Crash reports are used to understand and fix technical problems. They may include technical diagnostics such as stack traces, error messages, app version, device and operating system information, runtime information, timestamps, and related context. Depending on the error and the App's configuration, crash reports could include information that you entered into the App or that appears in technical logs. We aim to avoid sending workout content or directly identifying information unless needed to investigate the report or unless you choose to include it.

We configure crash reporting with privacy-conscious settings where possible, such as avoiding unnecessary personally identifying information, filtering sensitive fields, and limiting crash reports to information needed for debugging.

## 6. No Sale, Payments, Or Advertising

The current alpha version is free. We do not sell personal data, sell access to the App, process in-app payments, or use advertising in the App.

## 7. Legal Bases

Because the alpha is currently offered to testers in Austria and/or the European Union, the GDPR may apply. We process personal data based on one or more of the following legal bases:

- Performance of a contract, to provide the App and its features.
- Legitimate interests, such as maintaining and improving the App, handling support, and preventing misuse.
- Consent, where required, for example when you choose to send an optional crash report, or for optional analytics, marketing, or tracking.
- Legal obligations, where we must retain or disclose information by law.

## 8. Data Sharing

We do not sell personal data. We may share data:

- With service providers that help us operate, support, or improve the App.
- If required by law or a valid legal request.
- If necessary to protect our rights, users, or the security of the App.
- With your instruction or consent.

## 9. Retention And Deletion

Data stored locally remains on your device until you delete it, reset the App, or uninstall the App. We cannot delete local data from your device remotely because we do not operate a PEAK cloud account or sync service for the current alpha version.

Crash reports, support messages, feedback messages, and alpha distribution records are retained only as long as reasonably necessary for alpha testing, debugging, support, and legal compliance. As a default, we aim to delete or anonymize crash reports and feedback records within 12 months unless we need them longer for a specific issue or legal reason.

## 10. Your Rights

Depending on your location, you may have rights to access, correct, delete, restrict, object to, or receive a copy of your personal data. You may also have the right to withdraw consent where processing is based on consent.

To exercise your rights, contact privacy@solid-software.studio. If you are in the European Economic Area, you may also have the right to lodge a complaint with your local data protection authority.

## 11. Children

The App is not intended for children under 16. We do not knowingly collect personal data from children under this age.

## 12. International Transfers

If data is processed outside your country, we take appropriate measures intended to protect it according to applicable law.

## 13. Changes

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. The updated version will be indicated by a new "Last updated" date.
